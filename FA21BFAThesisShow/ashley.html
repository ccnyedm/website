Ashley Montero

Think Twice

Process Book

Abstract: Think Twice is a graphic novel that features stories about different interpersonal situations where a false assumption is made. These stories are inspired by actual events told in interviews by family members, peers, and news articles. The graphic novel contains five different examples of someone making an assumption toward another person, with consequences, which will hopefully serve as lessons for the audience and a way to identify the 12 universal types of assumptions, listed in the beginning of graphic novel and on the supporting website. The primary audience member for this project are teenagers, aged 13 to 19, because that’s the age range where young adults are most ready to learn learn and adapt to their environment. To promote the graphic novel, I’ve created a website, a set of five educational posters, a set of five bookmarks, pins, an Instagram account, and a 48-second promotional video.

My research goal is to understand the psychological reason why one makes assumptions. Most of the time, assumptions are made without a second thought given—which often leads to negative consequences and a lasting impact on one’s life—and are not usually based on tangible facts or observable evidence. Sometimes, we let our own prejudices influence the way we behave towards certain people. This can cause a person to treat someone negatively based on whatever preconceived notion they may have towards them. This behavior can perpetuate stereotypes, prejudice, and discrimination that impacts mental health and can ultimately lead to anxiety, depression, or post-traumatic stress disorder.

Project Elements

Graphic Novel

Think Twice is a hardcover 5.5 x 8.5-inch graphic novel that contains five chapters with different situations where a false assumption is made in everyday life leading to consequences after they are made. There is a different set of characters for each chapter.



Promotional Video

A 48-second video advertises the graphic novel.


Graphic Novel Storyboard

This storyboard features every page and chapter breakdown from the graphic novel, Think Twice. Chapter 1 is about a store clerk who learns the hard way that you can’t judge a book by its cover. Chapter 2 is about a young woman who gets shamed on the bus by two presumptuous older women who assume she is a teenage mother. Chapter 3 is about a minority doctor who faces discrimination from a taxi driver and learns that we are all connected in the most unexpected ways. Chapter 4 is about a woman’s racial bias regarding her property, leading to a surprising confrontation. Chapter 5 is about a woman’s derogatory remarks that lead to an embarrassing encounter on the subway.

Character Designs

The characters were first sketched out with paper and pencil and then generated using a painting software called FireAlpaca, which allowed for organic lines to mirror the strokes of traditional drawing tools such as pen, pencil, and paint.


Website

The Think Twice website promotes the graphic novel and educates users about assumptions. It also features the promotional video, which advertises the graphic novel’s launch and invites interested audience members to purchase the book.

To view the website, go to https://ttproj.wixsite.com/thinktwice

Posters

Five 24 x 36-inch posters feature scenes from each chapter in the graphic novel. The different colors on each poster represent the chapter from which they are taken. Their purpose is to provide tips on the ways you can catch yourself before making an assumption about someone.

Pins

Ten 2.25-inch pins feature characters from the graphic novel.


Bookmarks

Five 2 x 6-inch bookmarks follow the same format as the posters. Their purpose is to provide tips on the ways you can catch yourself before making an assumption about someone.


Instagram

Think Twice uses Instagram to promote the graphic novel and invites users to submit their own false assumption stories.

Link to Instagram: https://www.instagram.com/think.twice.project
