Electronic Design & Multimedia • Fall 2021 BFA Thesis Show

Online Exhibition, opening December 13th, 2021

This event is made possible by Electronic Design & Multimedia (EDM) and the Robinson Center for Graphic Arts and Communication Design, within the Art Department at The City College of New York (CUNY).

Fall 2021 projects include: a graphic novel that encourages us to think twice before making an assumption, inspired by actual events told in interviews by family members, peers, and news articles • an illustration and activism-based journey through New York City’s Chinatown district, in effort to revitalize the neighborhood as we emerge from the pandemic • a branding and information design project that promotes and encourage weightlifting for women while educating gym-goers on gender inequalities within the fitness industry • a branding and packaging project that provides mental wellness to tea drinkers through Latin American herbal-tea remedies packaged within a contemporary design

Student Projects

Allen Guzman: Athenic

Athenic is a branding project for a women-centered weightlifting gym aimed to inform, inspire, and initiate change regarding the gender inequalities women face in weight rooms.

Sandy Huang: HUA

HUA is an activism and illustration project emerging from a desire to preserve the history of Chinatown and promote the people within the community. HUA’s goal is to use storytelling and a fun interactive map tour to help preserve, promote, and enrich the neighborhood. It is a love letter to a place that has been greatly impacted by the current COVID-19 pandemic.

Ashley Montero: Think Twice

Think Twice is an illustrated graphic novel that features five different impersonal situations where a false assumption is made. These stories are inspired by actual events with the goal of educating the reader on how to think twice before making an assumption.

Patricia Santana: SONRÍELE

SONRÍELE Herbal Tea Company is a Latino herbal tea packaging and branding project that reframes the ancestral herbal medicine knowledge of Latin America in a modern context. The brand promotes culturally and scientifically backed herbal tea blends to improve mood, energy, and sleep quality.





The BFA Thesis Show is an exhibition of capstone projects for the BFA in Electronic Design & Multimedia.
