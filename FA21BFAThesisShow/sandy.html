Sandy Huang

HUA

Process Book

Abstract: HUA is an activism and branding project emerging from a desire to preserve the history of Chinatown and promote the people within the community at the crossroads of the current COVID-19 pandemic. The project consists of a promotional box which includes an information card, postcards, three illustrated books, an illustrated map of Chinatown, a passport/booklet allowing visitors to collect custom stamps from different locations around the neighborhood, a poster, tote bag, sticker sheets and a button pin. HUA’s mission is to promote visiting Chinatown and increase foot traffic in the neighborhood following the upsetting 1.5 year since the start of Covid-19. The project is targeted towards people in the Generation Z and Millennial category – the most tech savvy and socially aware group to influence a greater visitation/business outcome for the community. The project aims to increase community engagement among people through an exciting and creative interactive box and acts as a great education tool to teach others about the neighborhood.

The pinyin, HUA, can be referred to the Chinese character 花, which means flower/blossom or the character 华 meaning magnificent and splendid, also used to refer to the Chinese ethnicity. The acronym HUA is also used in the US military for “heard, understood, acknowledged.”

Project Elements

Previous Next

Promotional Box

An 8 x 11-inch custom mailer box consist of three illustrated books, a map of Chinatown, a passport booklet, three postcards, sticker and pin pack, and tote bag wrapped in a custom design handkerchief. The box invites recipients to visit favorite restaurants and stores in Chinatown to both promote these locations and bring a greater awareness to the New York City neighborhood.



Three Illustrated Books

A 5 x 8-inch, three-part illustrated book series about Chinatown features three fictional characters—an elderly man, a working mom, and a college student—and tells the story of their contemporary lives in New York City’s Chinatown. Each book features real-life, local Chinatown shops that readers can visit and explore themselves after reading the stories.



Ye Ye’s Qing Ming Festival

The first book centers on an elderly man and focuses on the topic of change and the history of Chinatown—changes the community has gone through throughout the years. The old man in the story is recently widowed and is trying to navigate life without his partner of 60 years.



Ma Ma’s American Dream

The second book centers on a working mom and focuses on the topic of community and the relationships between the people of Chinatown. The woman in the story is an immigrant mother who works at a local bakery six days a week and has a with a five-year-old son named Ollie.



Xiao Hua Finds Love

The third book centers on a college student and focuses on the topic of growth within herself and the growth of her community. The college student has just ended a two-year relationship and is looking for ways to deal with her heartbreak.


Illustrated Map

A 21.5 x 11-inch illustrated map of Chinatown pairs with the illustrated books to encourage readers to explore places the characters visit in each book. The map features the different shops and stores mentioned in the books scattered throughout Chinatown. The shops include dim sum restaurants, bakeries, tofu shops, dessert places, gift shops and entertainment centers.




Chinatown Passport

A 3 x 4.5-inch passport booklet is used to gamify the experience of visiting Chinatown. The Chinatown passport booklet is used to show proof of pilgrimage to specific destinations in Chinatown by receiving a custom stamp of the locations visited. Once the booklet is completed and filled with stamps, you are rewarded with a special HUA T-shirt of choice at any of the shops featured in the illustrated books.

T-shirt

A choice of three, limited-edition HUA T-Shirts are awarded after completion of the Chinatown passport booklet. Each t-shirt features a character from the book series with a line of positive affirmation printed on the back of the t-shirt. The prizewinner has the option to choose from the three t-shirts which one they identify with the most.


Other Box Contents

Info Card

An info card addresses the purpose of the project and the contents that are included within the box.

Handkerchief

A custom-printed handkerchief wraps the contents inside the promotional box. The handkerchief will be used in place of wrapping/tissue paper and can be repurposed as a face mask, head bandana, or a scarf.


Poster

A 10 x 13-inch poster features illustrations from all three books

Postcards

A set of three postcards features a different set of illustrations from the three books.


Tote Bag

A small, screen-printed, canvas tote bag is used as a walking advertisement for the project.

Buttons

A 3-inch pin with the project logo is also used as a walking advertisement for the project.


Sticker Sheets

Each 4 x 6-inch sticker sheet is dedicated to one of the stories from the illustrated-book series. The stickers feature all the locations mentioned in the book and is a good way to advertise the locations.


Website

A website advertises the promotional box. It consists of links to a mission page, a shopping page, an information page, and contact page. The website also features the locations mentioned in the illustrated book to further promote the community.
