Patricia Santana

SONRÍELE Herbal Tea Company

Process Book

Abstract: SONRÍELE, which means “to smile at” in Spanish, is a Latino tea brand that uses scientifically backed, medicinal herb blends of yerba mate, passionflower, and yerba buena to improve the mood, energy, and sleep of the new generation of tea drinkers in their twenties. I have created this tea company so that it is a celebration of the culture, but also the implementation of ancestral herbal medicine of Latin America in a modern context. Within my SONRÍELE brand, I have designed packaging for three tea blends, a tea sampler kit, a subway poster campaign, Instagram posts, a responsive website design, a brand book, and a case study.

According to reports from Allied Market Research and the U.S. Small Business Association, the tea industry is expected to grow to $68,950 million by 2027, but only 600,000 of the 12.2 million business owners in the United States are U.S.-born Latinos. Many herbal teas and supplements have been shown to have beneficial effects on mental health and are of easier access to individuals who might not have health insurance or legal status such as in the Latino population. According to the National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI), 34% of Hispanic/Latinx adults with mental illness receive treatment each year compared to the U.S. average of 45%. In addition, 19% of Hispanic people had no form of health insurance in combination with 5.7% of Hispanic/Latinx people in the U.S. living in poverty (compared to 7.3% of non-Hispanic whites) Individuals without clear access to mental health services are more likely to look for effective alternatives, such as herbal teas and supplements.
