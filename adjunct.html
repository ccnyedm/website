Instructor openings in Electronic Design & Multimedia, SP17

Special Topics Course: Introduction to UX/UI Design

COURSE DESCRIPTION

An introduction to user experience and user interface design for web and interactive media. Students will gain experience with UX methodologies, experience in designing UI for web and mobile, learn about information architecture, rapid prototyping, project planning and usability testing. This course combines elements of graphic design and front-end (web design). Prereqs: 29510 Electronic Design 1 and 39540 Design for the Web 1. 3hrs/3cr. Lab fee $40.

‪JOB DESCRIPTION

We are looking for a designer with several years of experience in digital design, specific experience in UX, UI and IxD as well as a good knowledge of HTML/CSS/JS; experience in implementing UX methodologies, UI design for web and mobile, proficiency in HTML/CSS, wireframing and prototyping. We prefer teaching experience at the college level, but will consider designers with appropriate industry experience who are interested in teaching.

This class meets Friday 2-4:50. Spring session at CCNY runs from February to mid-May, 14 classes plus 1 final review class.

CUNY adjuncts must either be US citizens or have a green card (permission to work in the US). The adjunct rate is approximately $72/hr for 45 hours (which includes the Final Review session).

LEARNING OUTCOMES:

Students will learn the core concepts and methodologies of User Experience and User Interface Design through lecture and studio practice.

Students will develop technical skills and acquire critical language in thinking about UX/UI Design and be introduced to common industry tools such as Sketch.

Students will learn how to integrate testing, prototype design and usability testing in the design and production process.

Students will gain skills in creative problem solving, innovation, and human-centered design.​

Students will build a portfolio with projects that employ personas, wireframes, usability tests and prototypes.

2D Imaging and Illustration

‪COURSE DESCRIPTION

Electronic illustration and image processing with an overview of approaches from painting to montage. Exploring imaging techniques through the use of masks, channels, filters and special effects. Issues of color management, color correction, resolution, and printing. Prereq: Art 10100 2D Design and Art 29520 Illustration. 3hrs/3cr. Lab fee $40.

‪JOB DESCRIPTION

We are looking for a an artist or designer with deep PhotoShop/image creation and processing experience, and including knowlege of preparing files for medium and large-format archival printing. A highly conceptual approach to imagemaking using digital tools is desirable. We prefer teaching experience at the college level, but will consider artists or designers with appropriate experience who are interested in teaching.

This class meets Saturday 9-11:50. Spring session at CCNY runs from February to mid-May, 14 classes plus 1 final review class.

CUNY adjuncts must either be US citizens or have a green card (permission to work in the US). The adjunct rate is approximately $72/hr for 45 hours (which includes the Final Review session).

LEARNING OUTCOMES:

Articulate the conceptual basis of their work in class.

Demonstrate the ability to critique the work of others in class.

Produce artwork using the latest software and technologies.

Develop approaches to creative expression that include risk taking, originality, and problem solving.

Demonstrate an advanced level of personal creative expression in at least one medium or a combination of media.

Students will build a portfolio with projects that demonstrate a personal approach to imagemaking and technical proficiency with image processing software, including a knowledge of color spaces, color correction, masking, retouching and compositing.

CONTACT:

Professor Annette Weintraub and include a current CV, brief description of your experience and proposed course outline.

ADDITIONAL DESCRIPTION:

The Art Department has a Digital Design concentration in the BA for studio art with about 145 students, and a BFA in Electronic Design and Multimedia with about 70 students, for whom this is an elective course. Students eligible to take this course will have taken typography, an intermediate digital design course and an introductory web design/production course.

The City College Art Department program in Electronic Design & Multimedia prepares students for careers in design in traditional print and emerging electronic media. The Electronic Design and Multimedia program at City College offers one of the most comprehensive, affordable 4-year programs in electronic art at a New York City public university, and has a 18-year record of alumni success.

Our students have found employment in advertising agencies, graphic design firms, magazines and newspapers as well as in the new media industries of web design, multimedia design, 3D design and animation.

For more information about the program: http://edm.arts.ccny.cuny.edu/edm/

For more information about City College including travel directions: http://www.ccny.cuny.edu
